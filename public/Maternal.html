National Health Programs

An overview of key health initiatives by the Government of India

Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakaram (JSSK)

JSSK launched on 1st June, 2011 entitles all pregnant women delivering in public health institutions to absolutely free and no expense delivery, including caesarean section.

Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK)

RKSK focuses on adolescent health through nutrition, reproductive health, mental health, and prevention of substance abuse.

Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)

This program aims to improve child health screening and early intervention services, covering birth defects, deficiencies, diseases, and developmental delays.

Universal Immunisation Programme

The UIP provides free immunization services to protect children and pregnant women against various vaccine-preventable diseases.

Mission Indradhanush (MI)

Mission Indradhanush enhances immunization coverage for children and pregnant women who missed vaccination in routine immunization programs.

Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)

JSY is a safe motherhood intervention under the National Health Mission, promoting institutional deliveries to reduce maternal and neonatal mortality.

Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA)

This initiative provides free antenatal services and special care to pregnant women on the 9th of every month.

Navjaat Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (NSSK)

NSSK focuses on basic newborn care and resuscitation techniques to reduce neonatal mortality.

National Programme for Family Planning

The family planning program aims to provide accessible and affordable reproductive health services and improve maternal and child health.

LaQshya Programme (Labour Room Quality Improvement Initiative)

LaQshya focuses on improving the quality of care in labour rooms and maternity operation theatres to enhance maternal and newborn outcomes.
